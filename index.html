Cleaning Sponge
**What's a Cleaning Sponge?**

A cleaning sponge is just a thing you use to soak up messes and scrub stuff. It's got holes that trap dirt and gunk when you're cleaning, whether you're using soap or not.

**Types of Sponges**

1.  **Cellulose Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Wood stuff.
    *   What they're like: Soft and soak up a lot, okay for the earth.
    *   What they're good for: Dishes, counters, most cleaning around the house.
    *   Don't use on: Raw meat juice cause it gets germs.
2.  **Scrub Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Foamy stuff with a scratchy side.
    *   What they're like: Soft on one side, rough on the other.
    *   What they're good for: Stuck-on stuff on pots and pans.
    *   Careful on: Non-stick stuff, it can scratch.

3. **Microfiber Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Tiny threads.
    *   What they're like: Super thirsty, doesn’t leave fuzz, can use over and over.
    *   What they're good for: Glass, mirrors, cars.
    *   Cool thing: Can clean without cleaners.

4.  **Melamine Sponges (Magic Erasers)**

    *   What they're made of: Special foam.
    *   What they're like: Like super-fine sandpaper.
    *   What they're good for: Walls, shoes, marks on tiles.
    *   Watch out: Can make shiny things dull if you scrub too hard.

5.  **Natural Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Sea stuff.
    *   What they're like: Okay for the earth, last a while, soft.
    *   What they're good for: Showers or just when you want to clean with natural stuff.
    *   Heads up: Rinse them and let them dry, they can get moldy.

6.  **Steel Wool/Metal Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Metal threads.
    *   What they're like: Scratchy, gets rust and grease off.
    *   What they're good for: Metal stuff.
    *   Careful: Will scratch delicate things.

7.  **Silicone Sponges**

    *   What they're made of: Silicone.
    *   What they're like: Don't hold germs, last a long time.
    *   What they're good for: Dishes, cleaning without germs.
    *   Not so great: Don’t soak up spills.

**Keep Your Sponge Clean**

*   Rinse it after you use it.
*   Get rid of germs:
    *   Zap it in the microwave (wet) for a minute (gets rid of most germs).
    *   Soak it in vinegar or bleach for 5 minutes.
    *   Run it through the dishwasher.
*   Let it dry out so it doesn't grow germs.

**When to Toss It**

*   Kitchen sponge: Every week or two (sooner if it smells).
*   Microfiber/silicone: Every few months (wash them).
*   Natural: Every month.

**Be Safe and Clean**

*   Don't use the same sponge for dishes and the bathroom.
*   Don't use sponges on meat juice, use paper towels.
*   Keep sponges just for certain areas.
*   Don't mix cleaners on one sponge.

**Help the Earth**

*   Get sponges that can be composted or are natural.
*   Use microfiber or silicone sponges that you can wash.
*   Make your own sponges.

**Fun Fact:**

Sponges soak up liquid because of little holes, the more holes, the better they absorb.
